Pollachi — The Green Gateway

Gateway to the Anamalai Tiger Reserve. A land of waterfalls, wildlife, misty hills, and one of Asia's largest coconut markets.

About

Pollachi, Tamil Nadu

Pollachi is a town in Coimbatore district, Tamil Nadu, nestled at the foothills of the Western Ghats. It is known as the "Gateway to Anamalai" and is famous for its rich biodiversity, agricultural abundance, and natural beauty.

The region is home to the sprawling Anamalai Tiger Reserve (958 sq km), the breathtaking Monkey Falls, the misty Valparai hill station perched at 3,500 feet, and the iconic Top Slip safari base. Pollachi is also one of Asia's largest coconut and vegetable wholesale markets, making it agriculturally significant.

Palani — The Sacred Summit

Home to the ancient Dhandayuthapani Swamy Temple atop the 152-meter Sivagiri Hill — one of the most revered Murugan shrines in all of India.

About

Palani, Tamil Nadu

Palani is a sacred pilgrimage town in Dindigul district, Tamil Nadu. It is most famous for the Arulmigu Dhandayuthapani Swamy Temple — one of the Arupadai Veedu (six sacred abodes) of Lord Murugan in Tamil Nadu.

The temple sits atop the 152-meter Sivagiri Hill, accessible via 693 stone steps, a ropeway, or a winch car. The idol of Dhandayuthapani is uniquely crafted from Navapashanam — nine poisonous minerals combined by the legendary Sage Bogar. The town draws millions of pilgrims during Thaipusam and Kanda Sashti festivals.
